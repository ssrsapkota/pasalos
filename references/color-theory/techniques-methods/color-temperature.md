# Color Temperature

**Keywords:** Warm, cool, balance, mood.

**Description:**
The perception of heat or coolness in a color. Every color can be warm or cool relative to its neighbors.

## Usage

### 1. Relative Temperature
*   **Warm Red:** Orange-Red (Fire).
*   **Cool Red:** Blue-Red (Berry).
*   Designers often shift the temperature of a shadow to be cooler (bluer) and the highlight to be warmer (yellower) to mimic sunlight.

### 2. Balancing a Composition
A 100% warm palette can feel overwhelming.
*   **Technique:** Introduce small "Cool" accents to relieve the eye.
*   *Example:* An Orange website with Slate Gray (Cool Neutral) typography.

### 3. Depth Perception
*   **Warm Colors:** Advance (Come closer).
*   **Cool Colors:** Recede (Go back).
*   *UI Tip:* Use Warm colors for buttons (Closest layer) and Cool colors for backgrounds (Furthest layer).
