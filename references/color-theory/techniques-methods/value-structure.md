# Value Structure

**Keywords:** Black-and-white, lightness, contrast, foundation.

**Description:**
The relative lightness or darkness of a color. Good design works comfortably in Grayscale (Black & White).

## Usage

### 1. The Squint Test
Squint your eyes at your design. If the layout disappears or becomes muddy, your value structure is broken.
*   Contrast should rely on **Value**, not **Hue**.

### 2. Value Bands
Limit your design to a few distinct value steps to ensure clarity:
*   **Band 1 (White):** Backgrounds.
*   **Band 2 (Light Gray):** Borders / Dividers.
*   **Band 3 (Medium Gray):** Secondary Text / Icons.
*   **Band 4 (Black/Dark Color):** Primary Text / Headers.

### 3. Colorblindness Protection
If your values are distinct, colorblind users will have no problem navigating your UI, regardless of the hues you picked.
