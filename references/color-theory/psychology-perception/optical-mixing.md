# Optical Mixing

**Keywords:** Pointillism, dithering, distance, blur.

**Description:**
When small dots of different colors are placed close together, the eye blends them into a new color.

## Usage

### 1. Text vs Background
Black text on a Yellow background will appear slightly "greenish" at the edges due to antialiasing.
*   **Fix:** Sometimes you need to tint the text slightly to counteract this perception.

### 2. Patterns
A pattern of thin Blue stripes on White will read as "Light Blue" from a distance.
*   **Utility:** Use this to create "lighter" versions of your brand color without actually introducing a new hex code, simply by using a hatch pattern or texture.

### 3. Gradients
Smooth gradients are essentially optical mixing of millions of steps. Ensure the midpoint color isn't "muddy" (gray/brown) when mixing complements.
