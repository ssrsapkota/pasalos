# Hover Color

**Description:**
A subtle or distinct shift in color when a user hovers over an interactive element. This is one of the most common and essential feedback mechanisms.

**Key Principles:**
- **Contrast**: Ensure the new color maintains sufficient contrast with the background and text.
- **Consistency**: Use a systematic approach (e.g., all primary buttons darken by 10% on hover).
- **Subtlety**: Hover states shouldn't be jarring; often a slight brightness or saturation adjustment is enough.

**Common Patterns:**
- **Darkening**: Slightly darkening the background color of a button.
- **Lightening**: Lightening a dark element to suggest it's becoming "active" or "lit up".
- **Opacity Change**: Increasing opacity from 80% to 100% on hover.
- **Inversion**: Swapping text and background colors (use carefully).
