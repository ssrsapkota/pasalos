# Serif + Sans Pair

**Keywords:** Contrast, Tradition, Authority, Elegance, Clarity.

**Description:**
The combination of a Serif and a Sans-serif font is the "breadcrumbs and butter" of typography. The Serif provides distinctiveness and authority (usually for headlines), while the Sans provides clarity and modernity (usually for navigation and UI), or vice-versa.

## Common Patterns

### 1. Serif Header / Sans Body
Traditional editorial look. High trust.
*   **Header:** Playfair Display, EB Garamond, Lora.
*   **Body:** Inter, Roboto, Lato.
*   **Vibe:** Sophisticated, Academic, Newsy.

### 2. Sans Header / Serif Body
Modern literary look. Invites reading.
*   **Header:** Oswald, Montserrat, Futura.
*   **Body:** Merriweather, crimson Text, Georgia.
*   **Vibe:** Medium, Blog, Storytelling.

## Tips
*   **Avoid Clashing:** Don't pair a very messy/grunge serif with a super clean sans unless that's the specific specific artistic intent.
*   **Watch the Mood:** A "cute" rounded sans (Nunito) clashes with a "severe" transitional serif (Times New Roman).
