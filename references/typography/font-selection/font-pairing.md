# Font Pairing

**Keywords:** Concordance, Contrast, Harmony, Mood, Diversity.

**Description:**
Font pairing is the art of combining two or more typefaces that work well together. The goal is to either create harmony (similar vibe) or contrast (distinct roles). Two fonts are usually enough; three is tricky; four is a crowd.

## Strategies

### 1. The Classic Pair (Serif + Sans)
Reliable and professional. The contrast in structure separates the roles clearly.
*   **Header:** Serif (e.g., Merriweather, Playfair Display).
*   **Body:** Sans-serif (e.g., Inter, Open Sans).

### 2. The Modern Pair (Sans + Sans)
Clean and minimalist. Requires careful weight differentiation.
*   **Header:** Geometric Sans (e.g., Montserrat, Oswald) - Bold/Black.
*   **Body:** Humanist Sans (e.g., Open Sans, Lato) - Regular.

### 3. The Concordant Pair (Same Mood)
Using fonts from different families that share a similar mood or history.
*   **Example:** A grotesque sans (Akzidenz) with a transitional serif (Baskerville). Both feel structured and "official".

## Usage Checklist
*   [ ] **Contrast:** Is there enough difference (weight, style, structure)?
*   [ ] **X-Height:** Do they have similar x-heights? (Helps visual flow).
*   [ ] **Mood:** Do they convey the same brand personality?
