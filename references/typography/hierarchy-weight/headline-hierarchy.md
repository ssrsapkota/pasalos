# Headline Hierarchy

**Keywords:** H1-H6, Semantic, Structure, Outline, Size.

**Description:**
Headlines provide the skeleton of the content. A clear hierarchy helps users scan the page and understand the nested relationship of information.

## Levels

### 1. H1 (Page Title)
*   **Count:** Only 1 per page.
*   **Purpose:** What is this page about?
*   **Style:** Largest, specific.

### 2. H2 (Major Sections)
*   **Count:** Multiple.
*   **Purpose:** Divides the page into thematic blocks.
*   **Style:** Big, usually with a border or spacing to separate sections.

### 3. H3 (Subsections)
*   **Count:** Multiple within H2s.
*   **Purpose:** Breaks down a section.
*   **Style:** Distinct, but closer to body text.

### 4. H4-H6 (Labels/Minor)
*   **Purpose:** UI Labels, card titles, deep nesting.
*   **Style:** Often uppercase, small, or bold. Can be smaller than body text if styled distinctively (e.g., eyebrow text).

## Styling Strategy
Use **Size**, **Weight**, and **Color** to distinguish.
*   **H1:** 2.5rem, Bold, Dark (900).
*   **H2:** 2rem, SemiBold, Dark (800).
*   **H3:** 1.5rem, Medium, Dark (700).
*   **H4:** 1rem, Bold, Uppercase, Gray (500).
