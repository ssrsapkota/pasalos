# Card Anatomy

## Structure
A card is a container that groups related information and actions. It typically consists of a header, body, and footer.

### Header
- **Purpose**: Identifies the card's content or action.
- **Content**: Title, subtitle, avatar, or icon.
- **Actions**: Menus, close buttons, or specific actions related to the card.

### Body
- **Purpose**: Displays the main content.
- **Content**: Text, images, data visualizations, or lists.
- **Flexibility**: Should adapt to various content types while maintaining consistent padding.

### Footer
- **Purpose**: Houses supplementary actions or status information.
- **Content**: Buttons, tags, timestamps, or social indicators.
- **Separation**: Often visually separated from the body by a divider or background color change.

## Implementation Guide
- Use semantic HTML (e.g., `<article>`, `<header>`, `<footer>`).
- consistent padding ensures visual balance.
- Ensure the entire card or specific action areas are accessible via keyboard navigation.
