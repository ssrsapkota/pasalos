# Card Borders

## Bordered vs. Borderless

### Bordered Cards
- **Usage**: High contrast separation, distinct grouping, or when shadows are too heavy for the aesthetic.
- **Style**: subtle 1px border.
- **Color**: Typically a neutral gray (e.g., `border-gray-200` in light mode, `border-gray-800` in dark mode).

### Borderless Cards
- **Usage**: Clean, modern look, often relies on background color contrast or shadows.
- **Grouping**: Works well when placing white cards on a light gray background.

## Hybrid Approaches
- **Hover Borders**: Borderless by default, gains a border on hover for definition.
- **Accent Borders**: A colored border on one side (e.g., left or top) to indicate status or category.

## Best Practices
- Ensure sufficient contrast ratio for borders if they are the primary boundary.
- Avoid combining heavy shadows AND heavy borders; choose one primary method of definition.
