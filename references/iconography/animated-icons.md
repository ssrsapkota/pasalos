# Animated Icons

> **Icons with motion states**

Animated icons use motion to communicate change, provide feedback, or delight the user.

## Characteristics
-   **State Transitions:** Morphing from one shape to another (e.g., Hamburger menu to 'X').
-   **Micro-interactions:** Subtle bounces, rotations, or fills on interaction.
-   **Looping:** Continuous motion for waiting states or attention-grabbing.

## Usage
-   **Feedback:** "Like" heart filling up, "Download" arrow turning into a checkmark.
-   **Navigation:** Menu toggles.
-   **Loading:**  Spinners, skeletons.
-   **Delight:**  Celebrating a completed task.

## Best Practices
-   **Speed:** Keep animations snappy (200-500ms).
-   **Purpose:** Don't animate just for the sake of it; it should aid understanding or feel rewarding.
-   **Performance:** Use SVG/CSS or Lottie/Rive for smooth, scalable animations.
-   **Respect Motion Settings:**  Disable or reduce motion for users with `prefers-reduced-motion`.
