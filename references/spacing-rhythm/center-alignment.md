# Center Alignment

**Concept:** When to center content.

Center alignment looks formal and classic, but can be harder to read for long blocks of text.

## When to use
- **Headlines:** Short, punchy titles.
- **Heroes:** Landing page introductions where the focus is singular.
- **Buttons:** Standalone calls to action.
- **Images/Media:** Often look best centered.

## When NOT to use
- **Long Text:** Paragraphs longer than 3 lines become hard to read because the eye loses its place when jumping back to the uneven starting edge of the next line.
- **Forms:** Labels and inputs usually work better left-aligned (or top-aligned) for faster scanning.

## Balance
Center alignment requires symmetry to feel stable. If you have a centered heading, usually the subtext and button below it should also be centered.
