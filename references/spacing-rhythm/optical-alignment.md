# Optical Alignment

**Concept:** Visual vs mathematical centering.

Mathematical centering often looks "off" to the human eye due to the visual weight of different shapes. Optical alignment corrects for this to make things *look* aligned.

## Common Corrections
- **Play Buttons (Triangles):** A triangle in a circle needs to be nudged to the right to look centered, because its visual center of mass is left of its geometric center.
- **Curved Letters (O, C):** Need to overshoot the baseline and cap height slightly to look the same size as flat letters (H, T).
- **Icons:** "Visual Center" is different from "Bounding Box Center". Use an icon set that is optically balanced, or manually adjust margin.

## Rule
"If it looks wrong, it is wrong." Trust your eye over the computed value.
