# Edge Alignment

**Concept:** Consistent left/right edges.

Strong edge alignment creates an invisible line that guides the eye down the page.

## The "Hard" Edge
- **Left Align:** In LTR languages, a strong left edge is the most legible layout. Paragraphs, headings, and lists should snap to this invisible line.
- **Justified Text:** Avoid on web. It creates "rivers" of white space unless you have sophisticated hyphenation engines (which browsers only partially support).
- **Hanging Bullets:** For lists, consider hanging the bullets *outside* the alignment line so the text itself maintains the hard edge.

## Right Edge
- Often used for numbers in tables (tabular figures) to make them comparable.
- Used for secondary actions or metadata.
