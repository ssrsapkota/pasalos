# Micro Whitespace

**Concept:** Small gaps between related items.

Micro whitespace handles the relationships between elements that belong together, such as lines of text, a label and its input, or an icon and its text.

## Details
- **Text:** Line height (leading) is the most common micro whitespace. Standard is usually 1.5 (150%). Headings can be tighter (1.1 - 1.2).
- **Kerning/Tracking:** Letter spacing. Tighter for headlines, looser for uppercase labels.
- **Margins:** The small margin between a paragraph and the next paragraph (e.g., `mb-4`).
- **Icons:** The space between an icon and its label (usually 8px or `gap-2`).

## Goal
To make the content readable and legible without the elements floating apart. They must clearly belong to the same group.
